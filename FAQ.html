Frequently Asked Questions

What is this?

Project No Dead-Zones's mission is to give RIT students access to wifi in places where it is currently unavailable: the shuttles. This is intended to allow more students to check in at shuttles while simultaneously providing a solution to their boredom.

How does it work?

Students wishing to use the wifi only need to login with the same information they would use on any other RIT site upon upon attempting to connect to the wifi. After that, you have access! Another purpose of this software is to assist with contact tracing.

How does contact tracing work?

Whenever a user's device connects to the shuttle wifi, the time at which the connection was made is logged on the NDZ servers, as well as the shuttle the user has connected to. The time at which the connection ends is also logged and used to keep track of which student potentially came in contact with each other on the shuttle.
